About The Company






PASSION HISTORY COMPETITVE PRICING
Most furniture today is mass produced, factory-made junk.
Perhaps the only benefit to this is a reduced price,
but at the cost of low quality products,reduced workforce
(machines replacing people) and a loss of age old skills.
 My name is Christopher Jasper. Ever since I
was a boy I have always been fascinated by blacksmithing and carpentry.
I began my career as a welder and fabricator but found my real
passion was for wood and carpentry,
living in the wooded hills of North Idaho. After first
beginning carpentry under Mr Jeremiah Leake,
I started my own business making caskets
(St Joseph's Caskets),going on to build my own house - by myself - and
then working under Gary Marston of Marston's Custom Cabinets
before transitioning into my own JASPER WOODWORK 		Most custom woodworking companies charge
a price that most cannot afford.
I price my services more competitively to save you money
without com-promising on quality design and construction.
